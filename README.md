# **Health Insurance Coverage Analysis**

## **Project Overview**
This repository contains an analysis of **health insurance coverage rates** before and after the **Affordable Care Act (ACA)** in the United States. The dataset includes state-level and national-level data on **uninsured rates, Medicaid and Medicare enrollment, employer-sponsored insurance, and marketplace coverage trends**. The goal of this project is to evaluate the impact of ACA on health coverage and identify key trends across different states.

## **Objectives**
- Analyze **changes in health insurance coverage before and after the ACA**
- Identify **states with the highest decline in uninsured rates**
- Examine **Medicaid and Medicare enrollment trends by state**
- Compare **employer-sponsored insurance vs. marketplace coverage**
- Provide an interactive **Power BI dashboard** showcasing key insights

## **Data Sources and Tools Used**
- **Dataset**: Health Insurance Coverage Dataset (compiled from the **US Department of Health and Human Services** and the **US Census Bureau**)
- **Tools Used**:
  - **Microsoft Excel**: Data cleaning and preprocessing
  - **Power BI Desktop**: Data visualization and dashboard creation

## **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**
### **Data Description**
The dataset provides historical health insurance coverage data across various categories. The key columns include:
- **State** – Name of the US state
- **Uninsured Rate (2010, 2015)** – Percentage of people without insurance before and after the ACA
- **Medicaid Enrollment (2013-2016)** – Number of people enrolled in Medicaid before and after ACA expansion
- **Medicare Enrollment (2016)** – Number of individuals enrolled in Medicare
- **Employer-Sponsored Coverage** – Number of people covered by employer health insurance
- **Marketplace Coverage (2016)** – Number of individuals who purchased insurance through the ACA marketplace
- **Marketplace Tax Credits (2016)** – Total tax credits provided to marketplace enrollees

## **Methodology**
### **Data Cleaning & Preparation**
- Handled missing values and standardized state names
- Created calculated columns to analyze **percentage changes in uninsured rates**
- Categorized states based on **Medicaid expansion and ACA marketplace adoption**

### **Data Analysis in Excel**
- Used Pivot Tables to summarize **uninsured rate changes across states**
- Examined **trends in employer vs. marketplace coverage**
- Analyzed **Medicaid and Medicare enrollment shifts**

### **Visualization & Insights in Power BI**
- Built an interactive dashboard summarizing key findings
- Created bar charts to compare **health coverage trends by state**
- Analyzed **the relationship between uninsured rates and Medicaid expansion**
- Evaluated **employer-sponsored insurance vs. marketplace adoption**

## **Key Insights & Interpretation**
### **1. Uninsured Rate Changes Before and After ACA**
- The ACA significantly **reduced uninsured rates**, especially in states that expanded Medicaid.
- States like **Wisconsin, Missouri, and Massachusetts** observed the highest decline in uninsured populations.

### **2. Medicaid Enrollment Trends (2013-2016)**
- **California, New York, and Washington** experienced the largest Medicaid enrollment increases.
- States with Medicaid expansion policies saw greater increases in insured populations.

### **3. Medicare Enrollment Trends (2016)**
- **California (5.8M), Florida (4.1M), and Texas (3.8M)** had the highest Medicare enrollments.
- Enrollment growth reflects an aging population requiring increased health coverage.

### **4. Employer vs. Marketplace Coverage**
- **California (19.6M), Texas (13.7M), and New York (10.9M)** had the highest employer-sponsored coverage.
- Marketplace coverage adoption was highest in **Florida, California, and Texas**, indicating reliance on ACA subsidies.

### **5. Marketplace Tax Credits Distribution (2016)**
- **Florida ($1.43M), California ($1.24M), and Texas ($0.91M)** received the highest ACA marketplace tax credits.
- Tax credits were essential in making marketplace coverage more affordable for individuals.

## **Data Story**
This dashboard was created to analyze the impact of **the Affordable Care Act (ACA)** on health insurance coverage in the United States. The goal is to provide insights that can assist **policymakers, healthcare professionals, and researchers** in evaluating **coverage expansion strategies and financial accessibility**.

### **Key Findings**
- **The ACA led to a substantial decline in uninsured rates, particularly in Medicaid expansion states.**
- **Medicaid enrollment increased significantly between 2013 and 2016.**
- **Employer-sponsored insurance remains dominant, but marketplace coverage plays a crucial role in bridging gaps.**
- **Marketplace tax credits made ACA coverage more affordable in key states.**

### **Conclusion**
To improve health insurance accessibility:
- **Encourage Medicaid expansion in remaining states to further reduce uninsured rates.**
- **Optimize employer-sponsored plans to complement ACA marketplace coverage.**
- **Ensure continued financial support for low-income individuals through tax credits.**
